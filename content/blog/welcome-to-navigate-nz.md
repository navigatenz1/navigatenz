When I was in Year 11, my mum asked me what "NCEA credits" meant. I didn&apos;t know either. Neither did anyone in our extended family. So we Googled, piece by piece, year by year — and a lot of things that should have been obvious weren&apos;t.

That gap is what Navigate NZ is trying to close.

## What we&apos;ve launched with

- **14 plain-language guides** covering NCEA, Cambridge, IB, University Entrance, subject selection, scholarships, StudyLink, and more.
- **5 interactive tools** (expanding to 9 in the next release): credit calculator, university matcher, scholarship finder, key dates timeline, personal statement helper.
- **12 action modules** — short checklists you can work through one step at a time.
- **A personalised pathway assessment** that takes 5 minutes and points you to what matters for *your* year level and situation.
- **Hindi and Chinese translations** of the main guides (with more on the way).

Everything is free. No ads. No data selling. No upsell to a paid tier because there is no paid tier.

## Who we&apos;re for

If you&apos;re a student or a family member in New Zealand who is figuring out the education system for the first time — this is for you. That covers a lot of households: migrant families, first-generation university-bound students, refugee-background families, anyone whose parents didn&apos;t go through the NZ system.

You don&apos;t have to sign up. Read any guide, use any tool. If you want a personalised pathway and progress tracking, the assessment + dashboard are there too.

## What&apos;s next

- **New tools**: university cost calculator, career pathways mapper, hall-vs-flatting comparison, pathway decision tree — all shipping soon.
- **School partnerships**: we&apos;re talking to a handful of NZ schools about getting Navigate NZ into careers advisors&apos; hands.
- **Volunteer team**: if you&apos;re a student, graphic designer, translator, writer, or just want to help — [join us](/volunteer).

## One ask

If Navigate NZ helped you or your family, [tell us your story](/share-your-story). We read every submission. Real stories from real families are how we decide what to build next — and they help convince schools and donors that this actually matters.
