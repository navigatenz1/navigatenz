In March 2026, the NZ Government confirmed that NCEA (the National Certificate of Educational Achievement) will be phased out and replaced over a three-year transition between 2028 and 2030.

Here is what that actually means for families right now.

## The quick version

- **If you&apos;re currently in Years 9–13**: current NCEA rules still apply to you. Your existing credits count. University Entrance requirements are unchanged.
- **If you have a sibling or child starting school in 2028 or later**: they&apos;ll be on the new system.
- **Nothing changes in 2026 or 2027.**

## The three-year transition

- **2028**: NCEA Level 1 is removed. Year 11 students do a new "Foundational Award" instead.
- **2029**: NCEA Level 2 is replaced by the NZ Certificate of Education (NZCE).
- **2030**: NCEA Level 3 is replaced by the NZ Advanced Certificate of Education (NZACE).

Alongside this, the Achieved / Merit / Excellence grade system is being replaced with A–E grades.

## Will universities still accept NCEA?

Yes — all NZ universities have already confirmed they&apos;ll continue to accept NCEA qualifications from students who completed them during the transition. University Entrance as you know it today still applies.

## What we recommend right now

1. **Don&apos;t change what you&apos;re doing.** If you&apos;re mid-NCEA, finish NCEA.
2. **Read [our NCEA credits guide](/guides/understanding-ncea-credits)** so you&apos;re clear on what you need.
3. **Use [the credit calculator](/tools/credit-calculator)** to check if you&apos;re on track for Level 2, Level 3, and UE.
4. **Talk to your dean or careers advisor** once a year — schools get direct updates from NZQA that individual news articles don&apos;t.
